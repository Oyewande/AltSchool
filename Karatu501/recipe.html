Jollof Rice and Fried Plantain

Jollof Rice is a popular West African Dish dish made with rice cooked in a rich tomato sause, season with spices, and often served with crispy fried plantains It is a staple at many celebrations, family gatherings and everyday meals.

This recipe will guide you through making a delicious pot of Nigerian Jollof Rice, perfect for any occasion.

Recipe Information

Servings: 4 people

Preparation Time: 15 minutes

Cooking Time: 45 minutes

Total Time: 1 hour

Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 2 cups of short-grain parboiled rice
  • 1/4 cup of vegetable oil
  • 2 onions (1 chopped, 1 blended)
  • 2 cloves of garlic, minced
  • 1 teaspoon of ginger paste
  • 6 medium tomatoes (blended)
  • 3 red bell peppers (blended)
  • Optional: 1-2 scotch bonnet peppers for heat
  • 1 tablespoon of tomato paste
  • 2 cups of chicken or vegetable broth
  • 1 teaspoon of thyme
  • 1 teaspoon of curry powder
  • 2 seasoning cubes
  • Salt to taste
  • 2 ripe plantains, sliced
  • Vegetable oil for frying plantain

Instructions

  1. Rinse the rice under cold water until the water runs clear. Drain and set aside.
  2. In a large pot, heat the vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the chopped onions and sauté until translucent.
  3. Add the minced garlic and ginger paste, cooking for another minute until fragrant.
  4. Stir in the blended tomatoes, peppers and tomato paste, cooking for about 5-7 minutes until the mixture thickens.
  5. Add the rinsed rice to the pot, stirring well to coat the rice with the tomato mixture.
  6. Pour in the chicken or vegetable broth, thyme, curry powder, salt, and pepper. If using, add scotch bonnet peppers for heat.
  7. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat to low. Cover and simmer for about 30-35 minutes or until the rice is cooked and has absorbed all the liquid.
  8. Meanwhile, heat oil in a pan and fry the sliced plantain until golden brown. Drain excess oil on paper towels
  9. Fluff the rice with a fork and let it sit covered for an additional 5 minutes before serving.
  10. Serve hot with fried plantain and your choice of sides.

Tips

For the smoky party-style flavor, allow the rice to slightly burn at the bottom of the pot. Be careful so it does not overburn.

Nutrition Facts (per serving)

  • Calories: 520 kcal
  • Protein: 8g
  • Carbohydrates: 85g
  • Fat: 15g
